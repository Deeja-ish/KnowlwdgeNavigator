The Never-Ending Water Cycle

The water cycle is the continuous process by which water moves around, through, and above the Earth. It's how our planet's water supply is recycled and cleaned for all life to use.

1. Evaporation

**Evaporation** is the process where liquid water on the Earth's surface turns into a gas, called water vapor. The Sun's energy heats up water in oceans, lakes, and rivers, causing it to rise into the atmosphere.

2. Condensation

As water vapor rises, it cools down and changes back into tiny liquid water droplets or ice crystals. This process is called **condensation**. These tiny droplets clump together, forming clouds in the sky.

3. Precipitation

When the clouds become too full of water droplets, they release the water back to Earth in the form of rain, snow, sleet, or hail. This is known as **precipitation**.

4. Collection

After precipitation, water either soaks into the ground to become groundwater or flows over the ground as runoff. It eventually collects in rivers, lakes, oceans, and other bodies of water, ready for the cycle to begin again. This stage is called **collection**.
