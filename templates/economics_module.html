Scarcity: The Core Problem

At the heart of all economics is the concept of **scarcity**. This means that human wants are unlimited, but the resources available to satisfy them are limited. Because of this, we must make choices, and every choice has a cost.

Supply and Demand

One of the most fundamental concepts in economics is the relationship between **supply** and **demand**. Supply is how much of a good or service is available, while demand is how much people want it. When demand for something is high and supply is low, the price goes up. When supply is high and demand is low, the price goes down.

The Role of Money

Money serves three main purposes in an economy: it's a **medium of exchange**, a **unit of account**, and a **store of value**. It makes it easier to trade and measure the value of things without having to barter for goods and services.
