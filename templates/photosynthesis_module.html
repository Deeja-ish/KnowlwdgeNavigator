The Science of Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process used by plants, algae, and certain bacteria to turn sunlight into chemical energy. It's a fundamental process for all life on Earth.

The Recipe: Ingredients for Photosynthesis

For photosynthesis to occur, plants need three key ingredients:

  • Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$): Absorbed from the air through tiny pores in the leaves called stomata.
  • Water ($H_2O$): Absorbed from the soil through the plant's roots.
  • Sunlight: The energy source that powers the entire process.

The Kitchen: The Role of Chlorophyll

The "kitchen" where this all happens is a special pigment in plant cells called **chlorophyll**. Chlorophyll is what gives plants their green color and, most importantly, is responsible for absorbing sunlight.

The Product: Sugar and Oxygen

The final products of photosynthesis are:

  • Glucose (Sugar): A simple sugar ($C_6H_{12}O_6$) that the plant uses for food and energy.
  • Oxygen ($O_2$): A waste product for the plant, but essential for animal and human life. This is why we rely so heavily on plants!
