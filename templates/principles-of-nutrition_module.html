Principles of Nutrition

Nutrition is the study of how food and drink affect your body. It involves understanding the nutrients that are essential for growth, metabolism, and maintaining overall health.

1. The Macronutrients

**Macronutrients** are the major components of our food that our body needs in large amounts for energy. They include **Carbohydrates** (the body's main fuel source), **Proteins** (essential for building and repairing tissues), and **Fats** (important for energy storage and absorbing vitamins).

2. The Micronutrients

**Micronutrients** are vitamins and minerals that our body needs in much smaller amounts. **Vitamins** (like Vitamin C and D) are crucial for immune function and many bodily processes. **Minerals** (like calcium and iron) are vital for bone health and blood production.

3. The Importance of Hydration

Water is a vital nutrient. Staying **hydrated** is key for regulating body temperature, lubricating joints, transporting nutrients, and flushing out waste. Dehydration can lead to fatigue and poor concentration.

4. Balanced Diet and Meal Planning

A **balanced diet** involves eating a variety of foods from all the food groups to get all the necessary nutrients. **Meal planning** helps you make smart food choices, manage portion sizes, and ensure you're getting a consistent supply of nutrients throughout the day.
